What is Crochet?

Crochet is a type of needlecraft that uses a single hooked needle to interlock loops of yarn, thread, or other materials to make textiles.

Unlike knitting, which keeps several stitches open at once, crocheting completes each stitch before starting the next. This creates different textures and patterns from soft, lacy designs to thick, cozy fabrics.

Crocheters use different stitches, such as ‚Äúsc‚Äù (single crochet) and ‚Äúdc‚Äù (double crochet), to make many items like blankets, hats, scarves, bags, and even stuffed toys known as amigurumi.
